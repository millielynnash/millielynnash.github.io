T-Test

  • A statistical procedure designed to assess whether differences in the means of two groups reflect differences in their respective populations or are simply due to chance.

How to Trick SPSS to Report LCL or UCL for Directional Tests?

  • SPSS reports both one-sided and two-sided p-values, but only two-sided CI is reported.
  • To obtain one-sided upper or lower confidence limits, run a test doubling the ğ›¼ (alpha) level
    • This doe snot affect the p-values reported in the output.
  • Say if you are conducting a directional test a 95% confidence level and want to see LCL or UCL, you can change the confidence interval percentage from 95% to 90% in Options.
  • The lower end of reported 90% CI would be 95% LCL (Lower Control Limit), and the upper end of reported 90% CI would be 95% (Upper Control Limit).
    • Any variation detected inside the control limits probably occurred by chance. On the other hand, variation outside of the control limits likely occurred due to special causes. The upper control limit (UCL) and the lower control limit (LCL) serve as boundaries for expected deviation in data.

APA Writing Template (Negative Emotion)

  • The pre-video and post-video negative emotion scores were collected from a sample of 34 participants, with a pre-score of 4.80 (SD = 0.926) and a post-score mean of 4.60 (SD = 1.028). Thus, negative emotion changed by -0.20 (decrease) after the video. The paired-sample t-test was conducted to determine if this change was significantly smaller than zero (significant decrease after the video), and the results indicate that the post-score was significantly lower than the pre-score, t(33) = -2.393, p = 0.011. The null hypothesis was rejected at the .05 level of significance.
  • The effect size Cohen's d was -0.410. Using Cohen's (1988) guidelines, this is interpreted as a small to medium effect.
  • The results provide evidence to support the conclusion that the negative emotion significantly decreased after watching cat videos. The effect size suggests about 0.4 standard deviation unit difference between pre-score and post-score.

Independent t-Test (Comparing Two Groups)

  • Used for questions about whether the population standard mean (Âµ) from which we drew sample equals a specific hypothesized value (Âµ0).
  • Non-directional (two-sided) or directional (one-sided)
  • Population standard deviation (ğœ) is unknown: estimated by sample standard deviation (s).
  • Assumptions:
    • Observations come from a random sample, and subjects are independent of each other.
    • Variable is measured on at least an interval level scale.
    • Sampling distribution of the mean is normal, and CLT assures that for big samples (say 30).
