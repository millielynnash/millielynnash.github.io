Implicit Bias Training

Module 1: Understanding Implicit Bias:

What is Implicit Bias?:
  • Implicit Bias:
    • The attitudes or stereotypes that affect our understanding, actions, and decisions in an unconscious manner.
    • Biases can be neutral, positive, or negative.
    • Operating outside our conscious awareness.
    • Based on inaccurate information or stereotypes.
    • Implicit biases can impact our decisions, perceptions, and behaviors.
    • Actions and decisions resulting from our biases create real world barriers to equity and opportunity.
    • It is so important to learn about implicit bias, particularly those biases that don't align with your intentions or explicit beliefs.
    • Implicit bias does NOT refer to those beliefs we conceal or suppress in an effort to appear non-biased.
    • Implicit bias is a took we possess for understanding.
  • Stereotype:
    • Beliefs that are mentally associated with a given category.

Real World Implications:

Implicit Bias in Academic Achievement:
  • Teachers with strong, implicit pro-White biases were more likely to take a 'teach-to-the-test' approach.
Implicit Bias and School Discipline:
  • Discipline decisions are challenging and stressful for everyone involved.
    • Differences in discipline show up strongly with regards to race and gender.
  • Subjective or ambiguous situations can activate implicit associations.
  • Students of color are more likely to receive disciplinary measures for 'disruptive behavior', 'disrespect', and 'excessive noise.'
  • Culturally-influenced perceptions of student behavior.
  • Implicit bias can turn even our best intentions into unwanted outcomes.
Access and Evaluation:
