Key Terms

  • Cultural Competence
    • The awareness, knowledge and skills needed to function effectively with culturally diverse populations.
  • Cultural Humility
    • A complementary component to cultural competence associated with an open attitudinal stance or a multiculturally open orientation to work with diverse clients.
  • Cultural Responsiveness
    • A complementary component to cultural competence associated with an open attitudinal stance or a multiculturally open orientation to work with diverse clients.
  • Worldview
    • Composed of people's attitudes, values, and beliefs that affect how people think, define events, make decisions, and behave.

Cultural Competence as a Universal Necessity

  • Cultural competence is not just a majority group requirement
    • We must consider the impact of socialization processes on the development of biases.

Implicit Bias

  • Implicit:
    • Thoughts and feelings of which we are unaware
    • 'Mistaken about their nature'
  • Bias
    • A preference for (or aversion to) a person or group of people
  • A bias or prejudice that is present but not consciously held or recognized.
    • Attitudes toward people or associated stereotypes about people or groups that are not within our conscious knowledge.

Sociocultural Attuned Family Therapy

  • Sociocultural attunement refers not only to awareness of societal systems, culture, and power, but to willingness to pay close attention and be responsive to the experience of others.
  • Social Context:
    • Shared meanings that define culture, inform identities, and situate experience. Including the dynamic interconnections of social systems that shape constraints and opportunities within spatial settings.
  • Power:
    • A set of social process by which individual and collective interests are determined.
  • Third-Order Change:
    • A shift in relationship to sociocultural systems that expands possibilities and enables transformation of one's life.

6 Steps of Sociocultural Attunement

  1. Attune:
    • Understand, resonate with, and responding to experience within societal contexts.
  2. Name:
    • Identify what is unjust or has been overlooked (amplified silenced voices)
  3. Value:
    • Acknowledge the work of that which has been minimized or devalued
  4. Intervene: Support relational equity - disrupt oppressive power dynamics
  5. Envision: Provide a space to imagine just relational alternatives
  6. Transform: Collaborate to make what is imagined/real - Third-Order Change.
