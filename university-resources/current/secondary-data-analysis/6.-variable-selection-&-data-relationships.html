Selecting Covariates

  • What are covariates and why do we include them?
  • Conditioning on such risk factors is necessary to remove bias.
  • Do NOT use p-values to determine covariate inclusion. Use a priori theory!
  • Achieving statistical significance with covariates and without transparency

Issues of Measurement

  • As you are making decisions regarding your variables, consider the following questions:
    • How will constructs be operationalized?
    • What items will be used and which measures are critical to the tests?
    • If multiple measures are available, what will be done?
  • These issues often require quite a bit of conceptual considerations and defending in the peer-review process.

Measurement Problems

  • In big datasets, measures are often abbreviated.
  • Measurement problems arise when
    • The concept to be measured are vague or poorly defined
    • Indicators are imperfect measures of the underlying construct
    • Individual responses are inaccurate (e.g., distraction, illness, poor comprehension)
    • Errors lead to erroneous values
  • Accuracy
    • The closeness of the measurement to the true value
  • Bias
    • A systematic variation of measurements from the quality being measured
  • Precision
    • The closeness of repeated measurements to one another

Implications of Poor Measurement

  • Inaccurate and unreliable measurement results in serious concerns regarding the Validity and Reliability of the hypotheses tested using given measures.
  • The vast majority of constructs require more than one item, Why?
    • Low content validity (sensitivity and reliability)

Multiple Measures of the Same Construct

  • The primary objective of creating a series of composite scales is to avoid the exclusive use of, or dependence on, single item constructs where possible.
  • The use of several variables as indicators provides an opportunity to represent differing facets of a given concept, with the aim of yielding a more well-rounded perspective and, arguably, a better measure.

Correlation Measures the Strength of an Association

  • http://guessthecorrelation.com/​
    • Guess that correlation is a game with a purpose. It is entertaining, but data on our guesses are collected and used to analyze how we perceive correlations in scatterplots. So, the more people that play, the more data generated.
  • Always plot your data.

Plot Your Data for Outliers

  • Outliers are anomalous objects or values
    • Data that have characteristics different from most others
    • Values of an attribute that are unusual
  • This is not just noise! An outlier is an event that is suspected of not being generated by the same mechanisms as the rest of the data.

Global Outliers

  • Deviates significantly from the rest of the dataset

Contextual Outlier

  • Deviates significantly with respect to a specific context of the object

Collective Outlier

  • The individual data objects may not be outliers, but the object as a whole deviate significantly from the entire dataset.

Model Estimation: Non-Normal Data

  • Departures from normality are usually not serious in large samples, but there may be some bias in standard errors and test statistics.
  • One solution is to transform the non-normal data (e.g., log, square root, etc.)
    • Other methods are potentially more rigorous

Robust Standard Errors

  • Estimates of standard errors are robust against non-normality because assumptions of Normality are relaxed.
  • Several different ways to get robust standard errors.
  • Huber-White sandwich method, use the option vce (robust)

Reliability within Subpopulations

  • Why do we test for reliability in subpopulations?
    • Establish the psychometric properties of a new or existing measure/assessment tool
      • Means for determining generalizeability
      • Are we measuring the same construct in the same way across groups or across time?
    • Increase confidence that observed differences between groups reflect true differences and not those ties to measurement.
  • Reliability within subgroups is often assumed but rarely tested and is a big issue for many datasets that do not use previously validated meaures.

Using Existing Indices Created by Research Teams

  • Why use existing composites?
    • Data availability: you often may not have all necessary data to create the composite
    • Reproducibility: everyone uses the same construct
    • Convenience: time and effort spent on identifying possible composites and determining best source of information to creating them
  • But first, always read the user's guide and when item level data are available, check yourself!
