Central Limit Theorem (CLT)

Hypothesis Testing

  • Hypothesis testing is a decision-making process where two possible decisions are weighed in a statistical fashion. Hypotheses are written for each decision and then sample data are used to help make the decision.
  • Here we examine a statistical test to make inferences about a single mean with a known population standard deviation (ğœ):
    • One-sample t-test (6. One-Sample T-Test, Power)
    • Two samples, paired data: Paired (7. Paired-Sample T-Test)
    • Two samples, independent groups: Independent t-test (8. Independent T-Test, Homogeneity of Variance)

Types of Hypotheses

  • Null hypothesis (H0):
    • A statement about the value of an unknown population parameter. There is no difference or a 'null' difference between the population and hypothesized means (statistical hypothesis, chance model).
  • Alternative Hypothesis (H1 or Ha):
    • The opposite of H0. The scientific hypothesis that the researcher believes the outcome of the study will be, based on previous research and theory (research hypothesis, your theory).
  • Hypothesis testing in a nutshell:
    • Compare sample statistics to hypothesized distribution. If the data evidence is extreme, we can reject the null hypothesis and conclude that the event did not happen by chance. Instead, our theory is supported by data.
  1. Set up hypotheses based on the research question.
  2. Where does the sample/observed mean lie on the sampling distribution?
  3. Given the confidence level 100 (1 - ğ›¼)%, what is considered extreme evidence?
  4. Compared to the critical value is the sample mean extreme enough to be considered different from ğœ‡0?
