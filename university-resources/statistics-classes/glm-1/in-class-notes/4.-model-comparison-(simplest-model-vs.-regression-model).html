Assumptions of Regression

  1. Assumption of Normality of Error (Testing for Normality)
    • P-P Plot
  2. Assumption of Homoscedasticity (Testing Homoscedasticity)
    • Regression standardized residual scatter plot
  3. Assumptions of Independence of Error (Testing the Independence of Error)
    • Durbin-Watson test in 'model summary' of SPSS output
    • 1.5-2.5
    • Why? We want our data to be independent. Much of the time, our data is not independent. The data may be clustered or nested,
  4. Assumption of No Extreme Outliers (Testing for Extreme Outliers)
    • Cook's Distance in 'residual statistics' section of SPSS output
    • Below 1 to pas
  5. Assumption of Linearity (Testing Linearity)
    • Scatter plot
    • The Pearson correlation assumes that the relationship among x and y is a linear relationship.
    • ANOVA Table (Alternative test)
  • Note: the assumptions for any statistical analysis is about the outcome, not the predictors.
  • Assumption of Linearity
    • Scatterplot
    • ANOVA Table:
      • Deviation from linearity significance test, we want the value to not be linear
      • Deviation from Linearity: (Sum of Squares: 0.463) (df: 20) (Means Square: 0.023) (F: 1.226) (Sig: 0.320)

10 Steps of Model Comparison

  1. State the compact Model C and an augmented Model A.
  2. Identify the null hypothesis (H0).
  3. Count the number of parameters estimated by each model.
  4. Calculate the regression equation.
  5. Compute the total sum of squares (SSEC).
  6. Compute the sum of squares for Model A (SSEA).
  7. Compute sum of squares reduced (SSR).
  8. Compute the proportional reduction in error (PRE or R2).
  9. Complete the summary table.
  10. Decide about H0.
