3. Diagnostic Assessment

'Other Conditions That May Be a Focus of Clinical Attention'

  • Includes conditions and psychosocial or environmental problems that are not considered to be mental disorders but otherwise affect the diagnosis, course, prognosis, or treatment of an individual's mental disorder.
  • These conditions are presented with their corresponding codes from the ICD-10-CM (usually Z codes)
  • A condition or problem in this chapter may be coded with or without an accompanying mental disorder diagnosis:
    • If it is a reason for the current visit
    • If it helps to explain the need for a test, procedure, or treatment
    • If it plays a role in the initiation or exacerbation of a mental disorder
    • If it constitutes a problem that should be considered in the overall management plan
  • Suicidal behavior and nonsuicidal self-injury
  • Abuse and neglect
  • Relational problems (e.g., Relationship Distress with Spouse or Intimate Partner)
  • Educational, occupational, housing, and economic problems
  • Problems related to the social environment, interaction with the legal system, and other psychosocial, personal, and environmental circumstances (e.g., problems related to unwanted pregnancy being a victim of crime or terrorism)
  • Problems related to access to medical and other health care
  • Circumstances of personal history (e.g., personal history of psychological trauma)
  • Other health service encounters for counseling and medical advice (e.g., sex counseling)
  • Additional conditions or problems that may be a focus of clinical attention (e.g., wandering associated with a mental disorder, uncomplicated bereavement, phase of life problem)

Essentials of Diagnosis

  • The relationship comes first
  • Making a diagnosis is a team effort
  • Open-ended and check-list questions
  • Use screeners/questions to hone in on the diagnosis
  • Pay attention to clinical significance
  • Risk/Benefit analysis of diagnosis
  • Understand comorbidity
  • Be cautious about 'other' categories
  • Document your thinking
  • Remember the stakes are high
