3. EFT

Key Principles

  1. Collaborative alliance: models and provides a secure base.
  2. Emotion is primary in organizing attachment behaviora and the ways self and other are experienced in intimate relationships (primary and secondary emotions)
  3. Attachments are adaptive and healthy
  4. Problems are maintained by interaction patterns and by the dominant organization of emotional experience of each partner.
  5. Change occurs through new emotional experience in the context of attachment-salient interactions as they occur in session. (make it important to attachment, example: new parents, partners are not used to the difference in attachment and time spent in the relationship)
  6. The 'client' is the relationship.
  • Gas lighting is an attachment injury

Ultimate Goal

  • Creation of new cycles of secure bonding, which offer an antidote to negative cycles and positively redefine the nature of the relationship.

3 Tasks

  • Create a safe, collaborative alliance.
  • Access, reformulate, and expand the emotional responses that guide the couple's interaction.
  • Restructure those interactions in the direction of the accessibility and responsiveness that builds, secure lasting bonds.

9 Treatment Steps in 3 Stages

Stage 1: Cycle Deescalation

  • Step 1: Identify the relational conflict issues.
  • Step 2: Identify the negative interaction cycle
  • Step 3: Access the unacknowledged attachment oriented emotions underlying the interaction cycle.
  • Step 4: Reframe the problem in terms of the cycle, underlying emotions that accompany it, and attachment vulnerabilities and needs.

Stage 2: Changing Interactional Positions

  • Step 5: Promote identification with disowned attachment needs and aspects of self.
  • Step 6: Promote each partner's acceptance of the other's experience.
  • Step 7: Facilitate the expression of needs and wants to restructure the interactional based on new understandings and to create bonding events.

Step 3: Consolidation and Integration

  • Step 8: Facilitate the emergence of new solutions to old problems.
  • Step 9: Consolidate new positions and cycles of attachment behavior.

Intervention

  • Alliance
  • Commenting on the process, and interpreting with attachment language to draw out each partner's emotional experience.
  • Enactments with expanded emotional experiences.
  • Commenting on the experiences so that they can be integrated.
  • How can we help you process when you get loud?
