8. Experiential Family Therapy

Experiential Approaches to Family Therapy

  • Myth of Fusion:
    • Human's must come to grips with 'aloneness.' Once we accept our aloneness then we have to make meaning for our lives.
    • "We believe people who love us should 'just know' what we need."
    • We have to accept we are a self. Once we accept our aloneness, we must make meaning of our lives.
  • Freedom and Responsibility
  • Humanistic Psychology:
    • Humans are good and move toward growth if unhindered.

Experiential Theory

  • Whitaker and others claim to be a-theoretical. However, Whitaker was a systems theorist and used ideas from humanistic psychology and psychoanalytic theory to guide his therapy.
  • Jay Haley thought he could change a family behind a mirror by telling a therapist what to do. Experiential therapists did not believe this is true. They want the family to be open to their emotional experience and thoughts they are having.
  • Anti-Technician:
    • Rather than a-theoretical, Whitaker and other experiential therapists are anti-technician. Manipulation does not encourage open experiencing.

Big Picture of Whitaker

  • Through feeling expression, families are able to balance separateness and connectedness.

Normal Family Development

  • Always 'becoming' or moving forward, toward growth.
  • Emotions/affect are validated and nurtured. Rules create balance: respect for affect and getting along.
  • Permeable boundaries which encourage expression of intimacy needs, differentiation, bonding, conflict expression/resolution, problem solving, and playfulness.

Symptoms

  • Symptoms develop when affect is blocked, impulses are denied and feelings are suppressed.
  • Boundaries and processes are rigid: holding themselves together is more important than experiencing life.
  • Superficial experience of emotion, self-denial, lack of empathy, lack of commitment, inability to handle conflict
  • Little awareness, little sense of choice, flow of behavior is blocked because of unexpressed emotion.
  • Carl Whitaker suggested that there were 3 contexts to our symptoms:
    • 'Driven Crazy' in an interpersonal context in which person is belittled or disqualified.
    • 'Going Crazy' a state of temporary disorganization.
    • 'Acting Crazy' an adaptation to the context.

Intervention

  • Increased personal integrity, greater freedom of choice, less dependence, expanded experiencing.
  • Strengthening the family as a system by increasing connectedness within the nuclear family and across generations. Enhance ability to support the completion of developmental tasks.

Whitaker's Symbolic Experiential Approach

  • Battle for Structure
  • Battle for Initiative
  • Working Phase
  • Termination
  • Use of non-rational, symbol, and metaphor.
  • Use of self is paramount: any technique comes from the therapist's creativity.
  • Create a new therapeutic environment to alter the family's interaction in hopes of producing a different affective experience.
  • Interrupt rigid, repetitive cycles
  • Activate and allow for constructive anxiety or confusion.
  • Expand the presenting problem
  • Encourage and support any new decision (freedom to choose).
  • Create trans-generational boundaries.
  • Create a therapeutic supra-system in which the therapist and family are aligned, and the therapist still has the ability to differentiate (get in and get out).

Big Picture of Satir

Normal Family Development

Four Universals in Family Life
  • Self-Worth:
    • How people learn to be people
  • Communication:
    • The ways people use to work out meaning with one another (Placating, Blaming, Computing, Distracting, Leveling/Congruence)
      • Placating (peacekeeper)
      • Blamer (im not happy its your fault)
      • Computing (have to have a rational explanation, ultra rationale)
      • Distracting (acts out when there is stress, someone who uses humor)
      • Leveling/Congruence
  • Rules:
    • The rules people use for how they should feel and act, which eventually develop into the family system.
    • Freedom to comment:
      • What can you say
      • To whom can you say it
      • How do you go about it if you disagree or disapprove of someone or something
      • How do you question when you do not understand
      • Taboo subjects
      • Taboo feelings
      • Expressing affection
      • Secrets
    • Overarching goal may be to loosen up the system so there is freedom to comment. Maybe you say it yourself, maybe use family sculpting,
  • Link to Society:
    • How the family interacts with the outside world

Symptoms

  • When communication patterns are rigid (other than congruence) and continual, and you can't comment on that.
  • Symptoms are symptoms of the system
  • Symptoms are created through the process of interaction

Unhealthy Family Systems

  • Low self-worth
  • Communication is indirect, vague, not honest
  • Rules are rigid and non-negotiable
  • Link to society is fearful, placating, and blaming
