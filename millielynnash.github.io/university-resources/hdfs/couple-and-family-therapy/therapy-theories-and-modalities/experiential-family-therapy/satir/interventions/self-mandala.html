Self-Mandala

  • A concept whereby the person always has eight universal resources available to them: spiritual, physical, intellectual, emotional, sensual, contextual, nutritional, and interactional.
  • The individual is at the center, and surrounding them are the eight universal human resources: spiritual, contextual, nutritional, interactional, sensual, emotional, intellectual, and physical. (Reiter 2023)
  • Each part is interconnected. The less these 8 areas are given energy and time, the lower a person's Self-Esteem or Self-Worth. (Reiter 2023)

Spiritual

  • This has to do with your spiritual connection. This represents your relationship to your life force. How do you regard your life? Do you honor it? Do you use your spiritual connection in your daily life?

Contextual

  • This has to do with your context. This refers to the sights and sounds, the feel of things, the temperature, the light, the color, the quality of air, and the space where you live and work. Each of these factors has an important effect on your life.

Nutrition

  • This has to do with nutrition. What kind of food and drink do you put into your body?

Interactional

  • This shows how you interact with the people in your world. Your interaction shapes the nature of your relationships. How do you evaluate the quality of your various relationships? How do you use your power? Do you give it away and become a victim? Do you use it to become a dictator? Do you use it to develop harmony within yourself and with others, thus becoming an empowering leader? Do you use your power to nurture yourself or others, or to threaten? Do you team up with family members of others to do things together? Do you have fun, make jokes, and generally use good humor to make your life and that of others lighter and happier?
    • Remember that humor and love are two very powerful healing forces.

Sensual

  • This lets you know how you sense yourself. What is the physical condition of your sense organs? What freedoms do you give yourself to look, hear, touch, taste, and smell?
  • Many of us were taught that we could only look at, touch, or listen to certain things. That often resulted in limiting our use of our senses. To acknowledge, honor, and fully and freely use our senses is the main way we interact with the outside world and stimulate ourselves internally.

Emotional

  • This is that of your emotions, your feelings. How free are you to recognize and acknowledge them? What restrictions do you put on your feelings? How do you express them?
  • All feelings are human. They bring texture, color, and sensitivity to life. Without feelings, we would be robots.
  • Your feelings about these feelings show you where your self-worth is in relation to your feelings.

Intellectual

  • This is the cognitive part of your brain. This lens tells you about the information you take in, the thoughts you have, and the meaning you give them.
  • From this cognitive and rational part, you can get answers to such questions as, "What do I understand? How do I learn new things? How do I analyze situations and solve problems?"

Physical

  • This is the physical body with all its parts and systems.
  • Find a good anatomy book in which you can see pictures of your bones, muscles, internal organs, together with all the different systems. Let yourself be awed and amazed at how wonderfully your body is engineered and what a treasure house of resources it contains.
  • Apply this information to your own body. Are you, its owner, meeting its needs? Are you listening to it? It will tell you when you are tired, hungry, or over-stressed, or alert you to any part of you that needs special care.
